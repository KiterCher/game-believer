# H1: March 7th Build Guide — Best Relics, Light Cones & Teams | GameBeliever

## H2: March 7th Build Overview

March 7th is a versatile **4-star Ice Preservation** unit who excels at providing team-wide shields and dishing out significant counter-attack damage. As a tank from the Path of Preservation, her primary role is to enhance the team's survivability, but her unique kit allows her to function as a hybrid sub-DPS. She's one of the most accessible and effective tanks for new players and retains utility in endgame content like Memory of Chaos when built correctly.

**Build Philosophy:** This March 7th build focuses on maximizing the effectiveness of her shields while scaling her counter-attack damage, which is triggered by her Talent. We prioritize **DEF** to boost her shield strength and **CRIT Stats** to ensure her counters hit hard. This dual-purpose build allows her to protect the team while contributing meaningful damage, making her a valuable team member beyond just soaking hits.

**Why This Build Works:**
*   **Shield Scaling:** Her Skill's shield strength scales directly with her **DEF**. Higher DEF means beefier shields that can absorb more damage, reducing the burden on your healer.
*   **Damage Contribution:** Her Talent, **"The Artist in Motion,"** allows her to perform a powerful counter-attack when a shielded ally is hit. This counter scales with her **ATK** but benefits immensely from **CRIT** stats to land critical hits.
*   **Utility & Control:** Her Ultimate applies **Frozen** to enemies, providing crowd control (CC) that can interrupt deadly attacks and synergize with other Ice characters.

By building for both DEF and CRIT, March 7th becomes a self-sustaining tank who protects her team and punishes enemies for attacking them.

## H2: Best Relics for March 7th

### H3: Best 4pc Set
**Guard of Wuthering Snow** is the definitive 4-piece relic set for a hybrid shielder-DPS March 7th.
*   **Why:** The 2-piece bonus grants **DEF +15%**, directly increasing her shield strength. The 4-piece bonus reduces damage taken by 10% when HP is 50% or less, helping her survive focus fire and maintain her role as a tank.
*   **Set Bonus Explanation:** This set perfectly marries her survival and utility needs. The DEF boost is non-negotiable for shield scaling, while the damage reduction ensures she can stay on the field longer to apply more shields and counters.

### H3: Alternative Sets
If you lack a full Guard of Wuthering Snow set, consider these **2pc + 2pc** combinations:
*   **Guard of Wuthering Snow (2pc) + Knight of Purity Palace (2pc):** Provides a massive **DEF +30%** boost. This is the best alternative for pure shield strength, ideal if your team already has sufficient damage.
*   **Guard of Wuthering Snow (2pc) + Musketeer of Wild Wheat (2pc):** Offers **DEF +15%** and **ATK +12%**. Use this to balance her shield strength with her counter-attack damage.
*   **When to Use:** Choose the pure DEF set for hyper-difficulty content where survival is paramount. Choose the hybrid set for balanced performance in general content.

### H3: Best Planar Ornaments
*   **Best-in-Slot: Belobog of the Architects**
    *   **2pc Bonus:** Increases DEF by 15%. Additionally, increases DEF by another 15% when the wearer's Effect Hit Rate is 50% or higher.
    *   **Why:** This is her optimal ornament. It provides a huge DEF boost and synergizes with a main stat or substat-focused EHR build to reach the 50% threshold, significantly enhancing her shield and Ultimate's freeze chance.
*   **Alternative Options:**
    *   **Fleet of the Ageless:** Provides HP% and a team-wide ATK% buff. Good for a more supportive, tank-focused build.
    *   **Penacony, Land of the Dreams:** Increases Ultimate DMG by 25% and grants 5% Energy Regen. If you want to lean into her Ultimate's damage and freeze utility, this is a viable option.

### H3: Recommended Main Stats
*   **Body:** **CRIT Rate** or **CRIT DMG**. CRIT Rate is generally preferred to reach the 1:2 ratio and ensure consistent counter-attack damage.
*   **Feet:** **Speed (SPD)** is almost mandatory. More turns mean more Skill usage for shield application and more opportunities for her counter to trigger. **DEF%** is a fallback if you already have sufficient SPD from substats.
*   **Planar Sphere:** **DEF%**. This is the primary stat for boosting her shield strength.
*   **Link Rope:** **Energy Regeneration Rate (ERR)** or **DEF%**. ERR allows for more frequent Ultimate usage for crowd control. DEF% is the best pure defensive choice. Consider ERR if her freeze application is critical for your strategy.

### H3: Substats Priority
*   **Priority Order:** **SPD > CRIT Rate/DMG > DEF% > Effect Hit Rate (EHR)**
*   **Minimum Breakpoints:**
    *   **SPD:** Aim for at least **105-110** to ensure she acts frequently enough to refresh shields. 120+ is excellent.
    *   **CRIT Rate:** Aim for **50%+** to reliably land critical hits with her counters.
    *   **EHR:** **30-50%** is recommended to increase the base chance of her Ultimate applying **Frozen** and to activate the Belobog ornament bonus.

## H2: Stats Breakpoints & Targets

*   **CRIT Rate Target:** Aim for **50-70%**. This ensures her counter-attacks frequently crit, maximizing her sub-DPS potential.
*   **CRIT DMG Target:** Aim for **100-140%**. Maintain a 1:2 ratio with your CRIT Rate.
*   **SPD Breakpoints:** **105 minimum**, **120+ ideal**. Higher SPD lets her generate Skill Points faster and apply shields more often.
*   **DEF Target:** **2000+** for her shield to be meaningful. With a DEF% main stat on the sphere and substats, this is easily achievable.
*   **Effect Hit Rate (EHR) Target:** **30-50%** to reliably land Freeze with her Ultimate and to activate the Belobog ornament.
*   **Energy Regeneration Rate (ERR):** If using an ERR rope, aim to reach the **119.4%** breakpoint (with Fleet ornament or other sources) to reduce the number of hits needed for her 100-cost Ultimate.

*   **Freeze Control Team:** **March 7th + Gepard (if you have him) + Pela + Silver Wolf**. This team stacks DEF shred and Freeze. Gepard provides an additional shield, making the team nearly unkillable while they control and dismantle enemies.

## H2: Gameplay Tips & Rotation

*   **Skill Rotation:**
    1.  **Use Skill on the most vulnerable ally** (usually your main DPS or a squishy support) at the start of a wave or before a major enemy attack. Prioritize refreshing shields before they expire.
    2.  **Use Basic Attacks** when all allies are adequately shielded to generate Skill Points (SP) for your team.
    3.  **Use Ultimate** to apply **Frozen** to key targets, especially powerful elites or bosses about to use a devastating ability.
*   **Key Combos:**
    *   **Shield-then-Freeze:** Shield your team with Skill, then use Ultimate on the next turn to freeze enemies, buying time for your team to act freely.
    *   **Follow-Up Attack Trigger:** If using with Clara or other characters who provoke attacks (like Lynx's skill), ensure shields are up to trigger multiple counters.
*   **Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
    *   **Shielding the wrong target:** Always prioritize your main DPS or the character the enemy is targeting with single-hit attacks.
    *   **Ignoring SPD:** Building March 7th too slow can lead to her being unable to refresh shields in time, causing them to expire mid-fight.
    *   **Wasting Ultimate:** Don't use her Ultimate on a single, low-threat enemy. Save it for groups of elites or to interrupt a major boss mechanic.

## H2: FAQ

1.  **What is the best build for March 7th in HSR?**
    The best build is a hybrid shielder-DPS setup. Use a 4pc **Guard of Wuthering Snow** relic set with **DEF% on Sphere, SPD on Feet, and CRIT on Body**. Pair it with the **Belobog of the Architects** planar ornament and a Light Cone like **Moment of Victory** or **Day One of My New Life**.

2.  **What relics should I use for March 7th?**
    **Guard of Wuthering Snow (4pc)** is the recommended set. For alternatives, use a 2pc Guard + 2pc Knight of Purity Palace for pure DEF, or 2pc Guard + 2pc Musketeer for a balance of DEF and ATK.

3.  **What is March 7th's best light cone?**
    Her best Light Cone is the 5★ **Moment of Victory**. The best 4★ (and highly recommended) option is **Day One of My New Life**, which provides team-wide damage reduction.

4.  **What team is best for March 7th?**
    She excels in teams needing strong shielding and supplementary damage. A **Premium Team** with a hypercarry like Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae works great. A powerful **F2P Team** is March 7th + Hunt Trailblazer + Asta + Natasha. She also synergizes wonderfully with **Clara** in a follow-up attack team.

5.  **Is March 7th good in Honkai Star Rail?**
    Yes, March 7th is a very good character, especially for F2P players. She provides reliable shielding, valuable crowd control with her Freeze, and decent sub-DPS from her counters, making her a well-rounded Preservation unit.

6.  **What does March 7th scale with in HSR?**
    Her shields scale with her **DEF** stat. Her counter-attack damage scales with her **ATK** stat but benefits significantly from **CRIT Rate** and **CRIT DMG**.

7.  **Is March 7th a DPS or support?**
    She is a **Preservation (Tank/Support)** hybrid. Her primary role is to support the team with shields and utility, but she has a secondary, significant damage-dealing component through her counter-attacks.

8.  **What is March 7th's best F2P light cone?**
    The best F2P light cone is **Day One of My New Life**, a 4-star option. For completely free 3-star options, **Amber** or **Defense** can work temporarily.

9.  **How to build March 7th for maximum damage?**
    To maximize her counter-attack damage, prioritize **CRIT Rate/DMG** on Body, **ATK%** substats, and use the **Musketeer of Wild Wheat** relic set. However, this sacrifices her shielding utility and is not recommended over the balanced hybrid build.

10. **Is March 7th worth pulling in 2026?**
    As a free, story-reward character, you don't need to pull for her. If you're asking about investing in her through Eidolons or Light Cones, **yes**, she remains a valuable and versatile tank who can see use in various team compositions and endgame content.

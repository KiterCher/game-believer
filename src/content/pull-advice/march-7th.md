# Should You Pull March 7th? — Honkai Star Rail Pull Guide | GameBeliever

## Is March 7th Worth Pulling?

**TL;DR: Worth Pulling (Niche Value) / Situational Must-Pull for Specific Teams**

March 7th is not a universal meta-defining character, but she is a uniquely valuable 4-star tank with a specific, powerful niche. Her value skyrockets if you have or plan to pull for characters whose kits revolve around launching follow-up attacks. For players building a "follow-up" team, she transitions from a free story character to a premier support tank. For others, she remains a competent but replaceable shielder.

**Who should pull this character?** Primarily, players who own **Topaz & Numby**, **Clara**, or **Dr. Ratio** and are building a dedicated follow-up team. Also, early-game players or free-to-play accounts needing a reliable, low-investment Preservation unit with crowd control will find her invaluable. If you already have a built Fu Xuan or Gepard, her pull priority drops significantly unless you specifically want her follow-up synergy.

## March 7th Value Analysis: The Unsung Hero of Follow-Up

March 7th's value is uniquely tied to a single mechanic: **follow-up attacks**. In the broader meta of raw shield strength or damage mitigation, she is outclassed. However, her value proposition is entirely different and highly specialized.

*   **Current Meta Value:** In the specific "Follow-Up Attack" meta, she is S-tier. Her Skill, **"Dangerous Sweetheart,"** doesn't just provide a shield—it provides a **taunt and a conditional Counter-attack**. This means when the shielded ally is attacked, March 7th launches a follow-up attack. This single ability makes her the only tank in the game who *directly enables and fuels* another character's core mechanic. In a team with Topaz, Numby's advance forward is triggered more often. With Clara or Dr. Ratio, it provides an extra, reliable source of their damage.
*   **Future-Proof Assessment:** Her future is intrinsically linked to the future of follow-up attack characters. As long as HoYoverse releases characters who want to be hit or who benefit from frequent follow-ups, March 7th's stock will rise. She is a future-proof *niche support*, not a future-proof *universal tank*.
*   **Account Investment Priority:** For a new account, she is a **HIGH PRIORITY** build. She is free, requires minimal investment to function, and solves early survivability issues. For an established account, her priority is **CONDITIONAL**. She jumps to high priority the moment you pull a core follow-up attacker.
*   **Comparison with Alternatives:** Vs. a standard tank like **Gepard** (larger shields) or **Fu Xuan** (damage mitigation + crit rate buff), March offers less raw protection for the whole team but provides offensive utility (follow-ups) and reliable single-target freeze. She is not a direct competitor; she fills a different lane.

## Best Team Synergies: The Follow-Up Enabler

March 7th's teams are built around one central theme: **protecting and empowering the character who launches follow-up attacks.**

*   **The Quintessential Team:** **Topaz & Numby / Clara / Dr. Ratio + March 7th + Ruan Mei / Tingyun / Harmony Buffer + Flex Slot (Sustain/Buffer)**
    *   **March's Role:** She shields the designated attacker, taunts enemies to attack them (activating their counter/follow-up mechanics), and adds her own damage with her follow-up attack. This creates a beautiful feedback loop: more attacks on your ally = more follow-ups = more damage.
*   **Teams That Benefit:** Any single-target focused team appreciates her ability to freeze a high-priority target with her Ultimate. In early game or Pure Fiction, her AoE freeze can be a lifesaver.
*   **Teams She Enables:** She is the cornerstone of the **"Counter-attack"** and **"Follow-up"** archetypes. Without her or a similar taunt, characters like Clara are significantly less effective. She turns these characters from "good" to "great" by manipulating enemy AI.

## Investment Requirements: Low Cost, High Niche Reward

One of March 7th's greatest strengths is her accessibility.

*   **E0 Viable?** Absolutely. Her core kit—shield, taunt, follow-up trigger, and freeze—is fully functional at E0. She is a **perfectly viable 4-star tank at baseline.**
*   **Key Eidolons:** Her Eidolons are quality-of-life and scaling improvements, not mandatory.
    *   **E2:** Increases her follow-up attack damage. Nice DPS boost.
    *   **E4:** The real gem. Using her Skill now grants an effect that reduces the enemy's ATK. This makes her shield more effective defensively, addressing her main weakness. A significant upgrade.
    *   **E6:** Boosts her follow-up attack's Weakness Break efficiency. Excellent for break-focused teams.
*   **Light Cone Dependency:** Not at all. She is **extremely F2P friendly.**
    *   **Best-in-Slot (Premium):** **Moment of Victory** (Gepard's sig) for extra DEF and taunt chance.
    *   **Excellent F2P Option:** **"This Is Me!"** (from Battle Pass) or the free event LC **"We Are Wildfire"** are fantastic. Even a simple **"Amber"** or **"Defense"** 3-star cone works early on. Her scaling is with DEF, making many cones viable.
*   **F2P Friendly?** **S++ Tier.** She is free, has low eidolon dependency, works with almost any DEF-scaling Light Cone, and her best relic sets are farmable. She is the definition of a high-value, low-investment unit.

## Alternatives to March 7th

*   **Who fills a similar role?** **Gepard** (5-star, bigger shields, no follow-up) and **Tingyun** (4-star, not a tank, but often paired *with* a tank to buff the attacker). The most direct 4-star alternative for a "protect one unit" strategy is **Fu Xuan** (5-star), but she protects the whole team differently.
*   **When to choose alternatives?**
    *   Choose **Gepard** if you need to protect your *entire* team from AoE damage, not just one character.
    *   Choose **Fu Xuan** if you need universal damage mitigation and a crit rate buff.
    *   Choose **Fire Trailblazer** if you want a taunting Preservation unit with more personal damage and a team-wide minor shield.
    *   **Choose March 7th when your team's primary damage source is a follow-up attacker.**
*   **Cost Comparison:** March 7th is free. Gepard and Fu Xuan are 5-star characters requiring significant Stellar Jade investment. This cost difference is the biggest factor. You can have a fully functional March for zero premium currency.

## FAQ: Your March 7th Questions Answered

**1. What is the best build for March 7th in HSR?**
Prioritize **DEF%** on the body and feet. Planar Ornaments like **Belobog of the Architects** (for extra DEF when taunting) or **Fleet of the Ageless** (for team HP%) are excellent. Her relic sets are flexible: **Guard of Wuthering Snow** for personal survival, or **Knight of Purity Palace** to enhance her shields. Speed is a secondary consideration but welcome.

**2. What relics should I use for March 7th?**
As above, **Knight of Purity Palace 4-piece** is often best for shield strength. **Guard of Wuthering Snow** is great for survivability in harder content. For Planar Ornaments, **Belobog** or **Fleet** are top picks.

**3. What is March 7th's best light cone?**
Her best 5-star is **Moment of Victory**. For most players, the best accessible cones are **"This Is Me!"** (Battle Pass) or **"We Are Wildfire"** (event). Even the free 4-star **"Trend of the Universal Market"** is a solid choice.

**4. What team is best for March 7th?**
A team centered around follow-up attackers. The gold standard is **Topaz & Numby, Clara, or Dr. Ratio** as the main DPS, paired with March 7th. Fill the other slots with buffers (Ruan Mei, Tingyun) or a secondary sustain if needed.

**5. Is March 7th good in Honkai Star Rail?**
Yes, but **niche**. She is excellent for follow-up teams and early game content. She is good, not universally great, for general purpose use. Her "goodness" is context-dependent.

**6. What does March 7th scale with in HSR?**
Her shield strength, follow-up attack damage, and some trace effects scale directly with her **DEF (Defense)**. Building her with high DEF is key.

**7. Is March 7th a DPS or support?**
She is a **Support/Tank**. Her primary role is to protect an ally and enable their damage through her taunt and follow-up trigger. Her own personal damage is a minor bonus.

**8. What is March 7th's best F2P light cone?**
**"We Are Wildfire"** (from the Under the Blue Skies event, if you have it) or **"Amber"** (3-star) are excellent, easy-to-obtain options. **"We Are Wildfire"** is particularly good as it boosts DEF and provides team healing on ultimate use.

**9. How to build March 7th for maximum damage?**
You generally don't! Maximizing her damage would require mixing ATK% and crit stats, which severely undermines her shield strength and survivability, making her ineffective as a tank. If you want her follow-up to hit harder, increase her DEF and unlock Eidolons like E2 and E6.

**10. Is March 7th worth pulling in 2026?**
**Pulling** isn't the right term, as she's a free story character. **Building and investing in her** is absolutely worth it if you own a follow-up DPS. If that niche isn't relevant to your account, your resources are better spent on universal supports or your primary team's relics and Eidolons.
